Atomic Volume vs Atomic Weight - Evidence for Periodicity

üìú Historical Context

In 1869, Lothar Meyer independently discovered the periodic law by plotting atomic volumes against atomic weights.

The curve shows peaks at alkali metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs), providing visual evidence of periodicity.

1
Periodic Peaks
Alkali metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs) appear at peaks of the curve, showing maximum atomic volumes.
2
Transition Metal Valley
Transition metals (Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn) have minimum atomic volumes, forming valleys in the curve.
3
Progressive Increase
Peak heights increase with each period, as heavier alkali metals have larger atomic volumes.
